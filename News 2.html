How To Maintain Motorcycle Chain And Sprocket?


In order to enjoy the right performance of your motorcycle, it is mandatory to know the cleaning procedure of the chain and sprocket of the motorcycle. Just like any other motorcycle parts, the chains and the sprockets also need proper maintenance. This blog by the highly-rated motorcycle parts supplier in the Philippines is going to provide you with 8 step-by-step quick guides to clean and lubricate the motorcycle chains along with the sprockets. This entire process does not take more than 15-20 minutes.


Step-1


Find out the type of your chain:


At the very first step, you need to understand the pattern of your motorcycle chain. If it is nothing but a regular chain, it can be treated aggressively. But, if it is a sealed chain with rubber between the inner and outer links, the approach should be tender according to its nature.


Step-2


Take your motorcycle in the proper position:


Before you start the process, bring your motorcycle to the paddock stand. If you do not have a paddock stand, you can even take it on the kickstand.


Step-3


Examine the chain and the sprocket closely:


Check the master link and the chain wear in order to evaluate the actual status of your motorcycle chain. If you notice that the chain is not in good condition, you can replace it with a new one.


Step-4


Spray a cleaner on the chain:


Get a good cleaning spray for the chains. Do not opt for kerosene as kerosene does not do this task efficiently. Do not compromise with the cleaning. Spray it well on the chain and sprocket of your motorcycle.


Step-5


Scrub the chain wisely:


Now, with the help of a motorcycle chain cleaning brush, scrub the chain properly so that no dirt can remain there anymore.


Step-6


Respray the chain again:


After scrubbing the chain, your motorcycle needs to be resoaked with the cleaning spray.


Step-7


Dry out the chain:


The chain should be dried out properly before starting the process of lubrication.


Step-8


Lubricate the chain well:


At the final step, apply a good lubricant evenly on the chain. And, here you go! Your motorcycle chain is clean again. Regular maintenance of the chain and the sprocket helps the motorcycle to deliver the best possible performance to a rider.
